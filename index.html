🎓 Card Grading Guide & Reference ▼

Card grading is the process of having a professional third-party company evaluate, authenticate, and assign a numeric score to your card's condition. The card is then sealed in a tamper-evident plastic case ("slab"). Grading serves two purposes: protection of a valuable card and standardisation of condition for buyers and sellers.

📋 When is grading worth it?
✅ Worth Grading
  • Raw NM value exceeds ~$30–50 — grading fees ($20–$50+/card) need to make economic sense
  • Cards appearing to be PSA 9 or better — premium only materialises at high grades
  • Key inserts: Holograms, Autographs, Holofoils, Limited Editions — these carry the biggest grade multipliers
  • Autograph cards — grading authenticates the signature and dramatically increases buyer confidence
  • Cards you intend to sell at auction — slabs attract serious collector bidders
  • Your personal grails — protection & display value regardless of economics
❌ Skip Grading
  • Raw value under $20–25 — fees likely eat the premium entirely
  • Cards with any visible print defects, centering issues, or surface marks — will grade PSA 7 or lower
  • Common base cards from this era — abundant supply keeps graded premiums low
  • Cards you want to keep accessible — slabs are permanent and hard to remove safely
  • Cards already in VG or EX condition — grade ceiling likely PSA 7, minimal upside
💡 For your collection specifically: Use the Grading Type field to track whether each card is raw, PSA-graded, or BGS-graded. Start by identifying cards where qty > 1 (duplicates are prime grading candidates — keep one raw, slab the best copy). Then sort your Top 25 by value — any raw card over $40 in NM condition is worth a grading submission evaluation. Compare the Raw vs PSA 8 eBay prices to size the potential upside before submitting.

Grading companies: PSA (most liquid market), BGS/Beckett (strictest, sub-grades), SGC (fast turnaround, growing). Current turnaround times and fees vary — check their websites before submitting.
